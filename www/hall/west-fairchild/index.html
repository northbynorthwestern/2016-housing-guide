West Fairchild (International Studies Residential College)

1861 Sheridan Road

This South Campus residential college goes by many names: ISRC, International Studies Residential College, West Fairchild, Best Fairchild (if you ask a spirited ISRC resident), Worst Fairchild (if you ask a resident of its rival dorm, CRC/East Fairchild) and “What’s that?” (if you ask anyone who lives on North Campus, and probably a large percentage of South Campus residents too). Its naysayers say it is a decrepit building with absurdly tiny rooms and an anti-social atmosphere, but many of its residents love the sense of community, suite/lounge setup and abundant free food. It may not be the dorm for hardcore partiers or aspiring frat stars, but if you’re looking for a small, quiet South Campus dorm with a gigantic pineapple mascot, it may be the place for you. Plus, renovations over the past summer mean better furniture, new wood-laminate floors, and improved bedroom furniture to make life a little more bearable.

What residents say

“The community isn’t bad. I’m not really involved in it, but it’s there. It’s a good dorm for freshmen, especially if you’re looking for a smaller community, but I wouldn’t live here again.”

Caroline Gaglio, Weinberg freshman

“I like that it’s small. I think the community is really nice, you really get to know people. It’s a pretty social dorm, especially since we have the suites. The rooms are nice, and I like that there’s wood floors instead of carpet. ”

Maribel Fernandez, Weinberg freshman

“The location is a plus; it's really close to downtown Evanston, the library, and a couple dining halls. However, the facilities are not the best. The community isn't closely-knit, but you definitely can make friends if you want to. ”

Nicole Fang, McCormick freshman

What non-residents say

“I haven’t heard much about ISRC, even though I live right next to it. I guess it’s a social dorm, and their mascot is a pineapple, but that’s literally all I know. My dorm, CRC, has Super Smash Bros. tournaments with them, which seems like a cool cross-res college event.”

Jacob Stern, SoC sophomore

“I hear that's it's very similar to CRC, so it's not exactly a nice dorm but is more known for its community. There are a lot of journalism kids in there.”

Virginia Van Keuren, SoC junior

“I stayed in ISRC during my prospie visit, and it was a pretty nice and laid back dorm. I said I was going to live there, but didn’t. Whoops.”

Jintae Park, Bienen sophomore

“I stayed in ISRC during BioExcel, a summer program that I did before freshman year. The facilities are old, but the lounges are good for hanging out and studying in, and it’s nice that every floor has a kitchen.”

Christian Santos-Sanchez, McCormick sophomore
